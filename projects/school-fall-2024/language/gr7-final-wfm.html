This book report is about The Wee Free Men by Terry Pratchett. Sir Terence David John Pratchett OBE (28 April 1948 – 12 March 2015) was an English author, humorist, and satirist, best known for the Discworld series of 41 comic fantasy novels published between 1983 and 2015, and for the comedy novel Good Omens (1990), which he co-wrote with Neil Gaiman. Pratchett's first novel, The Carpet People, was published in 1971. The first Discworld novel, The Color of Magic, was published in 1983, after which Pratchett wrote an average of two books a year. The final Discworld novel, The Shepherd's Crown, was published in August 2015, five months after his death. [1] Terry Pratchett was knighted for his literary prowess and what he offered through his literature. The Wee Free Men is a fun book about a young girl who's [2] name is Tiffany, and who saved her younger brother from the Queen of the fey because nobody else would. In the story she traveled through the fey world of dreams and nightmares to find her little brother, and fought all kinds of nightmares and enemies on her way through.
This book has important topics in it such as "do the work" and stereotyping. Even though he has such important life lessons in his book he has a fun and engaging writing style and has funny language.

Some of the funny things about the book are things such as the names "No-As-Big-As-A-Medium-Sized-Jock-But-Bigger-Than-A-Wee-Jock-Jock" and "Rob Anybody". He also uses words and phrases based off of [3] Pictish language such as "Ach! Crivens!" or “Them as can do has to do for them as can't. And someone has to speak up for them as has no voices.” and[4] “Whut's the plan, Rob?" said one of them.
